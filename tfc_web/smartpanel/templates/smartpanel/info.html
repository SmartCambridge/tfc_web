Adaptive Cities SmartPanels display travel information in the lobbies of public buildings to inform travel choices.

These panels take advantage of the real-time processing capabilities of the Adaptive Cities platform to make up-to-the-minuite information available to the travelling public. Screens are geo-located to automatically display relevant information and can be further customised by local managers.

SmartPanels are under active development, but can currently display:

  • Bus positions and timings
  • Bus departure times
  • Train departure times
  • Google traffic maps
  • Weather forecasts
  • Twitter timelines (in particular traffic-related feeds)
  • Static messages
  • Live copies of other web pages

The Adaptive Cities program would be really interested to here your comments on these panels and on how they could be further developed. Please join in the conversation on the forum on the SmartCambridge.org web site or contact us at admin@smartcambridge.org.

The Adaptive Cities program is part of Smart Cambridge which is itself supported by The University of Cambridge, Connecting Cambridgeshire and Greater Cambridge partnership.

The SmaptPanel development makes use of a variety of third-party products and services, including:

  • Weather information from the Met Office DataPoint Service. Contains public sector information licensed under the Open Government Licence
  • Train departure information provided by National Rail Enquiries under a modified Open Government Licence
  • Bus timetable and stop details from the Traveline National Dataset and from the National Public Transport Access dataset (NaPTAN)
  • Map data from OpenStreetMap © OpenStreetMap contributors (see details)
  • Icons made by Google from www.flaticon.com is licensed by CC 3.0 BY
